The Short Version

How to use this tier

At a glance

  • This is the subacute phase, built on Tier 1. Enter only once Tier 1 is easy and symptom-free; now you start adding real load to the mid-back.
  • Lead every pull from the shoulder blade. Initiate from the scapula, not the hand — that's what recruits the lagging rhomboid. Give the weaker side an extra set.
  • Progress to Tier 3 when these are clean and pain-free. Movement is led by symptoms, not the calendar.
TIER 02

Five moves · Rebuild

The exercises

Default to the Tonal; band or dumbbell alternatives are noted where useful. Keep the ribs down throughout and train the weaker rhomboid side a touch harder.

01

Tonal Seated Cable Row, neutral grip

Targets
Rhomboids, mid-traps, lats — horizontal pulling strength. Tonal: handles or rope, bench optional.
Setup / How-to
Arms set low; sit facing the unit (bench optional) with neutral-grip handles. Row the elbows back, driving the squeeze from the shoulder blades; pause ~1 sec at the back; control the return. Moderate weight; Eccentric mode optional. Alt: seated band or single-arm dumbbell row.
Dosage
10–12 reps × 3.
Avoid
Leaning back / using momentum; shrugging; flaring the ribs.
Caution
Lead with the blade, not the hand — that's what recruits the lagging rhomboid.
02

Tonal Face Pull

Targets
Rhomboids, lower traps, rear delts, external rotators. Tonal: rope attachment.
Setup / How-to
Arms set high with the rope attachment. Pull the rope toward your eyes/forehead, elbows high, hands separating at the end; squeeze the blades together and down. Light-to-moderate weight, slow. Alt: band face pull anchored at eye height.
Dosage
12–15 reps × 3.
Avoid
Shrugging; too much weight; jutting the neck forward.
Caution
Ideal for the weak rhomboid / lower-trap pattern — prioritize control over load.
03

Quadruped Thoracic Rotation ("Open Book" on all fours)

Targets
Thoracic rotation mobility. Bodyweight.
Setup / How-to
On hands and knees, one hand behind the head. Rotate that elbow down and under the body, then open up rotating the elbow toward the ceiling, following with the eyes. Drive the motion from the mid-back.
Dosage
8–10 per side.
Avoid
Forcing end range; rotating from the low back; holding the breath.
Caution
Build rotation gradually around T6–T7 — smooth and pain-free.
04

Tonal Single-Arm Cable Row (anti-rotation)

Targets
Unilateral rhomboid / mid-back; resists trunk rotation (core). Tonal: single handle.
Setup / How-to
One arm set mid, single handle, staggered stance. Row one elbow back while keeping the torso square — don't let the body twist. Squeeze the blade; slow return. Train the weaker side first. Alt: single-arm band or dumbbell row, bracing against rotation.
Dosage
10–12 reps × 2–3 per side.
Avoid
Trunk rotation; shrugging; momentum.
Caution
Compare sides — give the side with the weaker rhomboid an extra set.
05

Wall Slides (scapular upward rotation + thoracic extension)

Targets
Scapular control, lower traps, thoracic extension. Bodyweight; Tonal alt: light overhead press-out.
Setup / How-to
Stand with back and arms against a wall in a "goalpost." Slide the arms overhead keeping wrists/elbows on the wall and ribs down, then return. Feel the mid-back work.
Dosage
10–12 reps × 2.
Avoid
Rib flare / low-back arch; arms peeling off the wall; shrugging.
Caution
Only go as high as you can keep the ribs down — protects the costochondral joints.

Read before every session

Safety

  • Enter this tier only when Tier 1 is easy and symptom-free.
  • Lead every pull from the shoulder blade — that's how the weak rhomboid gets the work.
  • Keep ribs down; no rib flare or breath-holding (protects the costochondral joints).
  • Rebuild rotation and extension gradually and pain-free around T6–T7.
  • Progress is led by symptoms, not the calendar — a flare means back off that move.
  • Compare sides; favor the weaker rhomboid with an extra set.

A note before you start

This plan is educational and is not a substitute for a clinician's assessment. Progress by symptoms, not the calendar — if a movement provokes pain or a flare, back off and rebuild. When in doubt, slow down and let symptoms lead.
