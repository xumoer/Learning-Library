Unified Dice System

A d10 dice-pool RPG of lean pools, assured skill, and abilities pushed until the Entropy Die turns on you.

Core Rulebook · v5.1

Unified Dice System — Game Rules

VERSION 5.1 — CURRENT AUTHORITATIVE RULESET Supersedes GAME_RULES.md (v1), GAME_RULES_v3.md (v3), and UDS_PATCH_v4.md (v4 patch). 5.1 replaces v5's dice-stacking growth with the Power Ratio + Assured Success structure — pools stay small at every level, and bonuses scale through Assured Successes, rerolls, flat damage, and Margin instead of ever-bigger handfuls of dice. 1. Rules Vocabulary

  • Pool: How many d10s you roll.
  • Success Threshold (ST): The minimum face value on a die that counts as a success. Lower is better. Set by your finesse attribute (DEX, INT, or CHA).
  • Success: Any die ≥ Success Threshold.
  • Critical Success: A natural 10. Counts as 2 Successes and triggers crit riders.
  • Assured Success (AS): A guaranteed success added to your roll's total. Counts exactly like a rolled success — toward Evasion, Resolve, Difficulty, and Margin. The one limit: AS alone can never carry an action. You must roll at least one real success for AS to apply at all (see Global Rules).
  • Evasion: Successes an attacker needs to land a hit on you.
  • Resolve: Successes a mental or social attack needs to affect you.
  • Margin: Successes (rolled + AS) beyond what you needed, capped at AR/2 (min 1, max 4). Margin adds to damage and powers stunts.
  • Armor: Flat reduction to Physical damage after a hit lands.
  • Magic Resistance (MR): Flat reduction to Elemental and Magical damage after a hit lands.
  • Flat Damage: Fixed damage from a weapon tier or purchased ability effect. Applied only after a hit lands.
  • Reroll: Pick up one or more failed dice and roll them again; the new result stands. The pre-roll bonus that does not grow the pool.
  • Grit: Short-term endurance. Damage goes here first. Restores every scene.
  • Health: Lasting harm. Damage goes here when Grit is 0. At 0 Health you are Dying.
  • Entropy Die: A die tracking how stable an ability is. Steps down on a bad roll. Backlash when the d4 fails.

The Three-Currency Rule. Every bonus in this system is exactly one of three things: dice (added to the pool before you roll — see the Pool rules), Assured Successes (counted with the roll, but only if you land ≥1 real success), or flat damage (added after a hit lands). Rerolls are a fourth, narrow lever that improves a roll without enlarging the pool. Nothing else adds dice beyond your attribute and one point of situational swing — if a feat, trait, condition, or homebrew effect would "give dice," it grants an AS, a reroll, or flat damage instead. This is what keeps pools small at every character level.

2. Core Resolution

One engine resolves everything: attacks, spells, persuasion, climbing, lockpicking.

2.1 Build the Roll

  1. Identify the action type: * Physical (attacks, athletics, movement, grapples) * Magical / Mental (spells, abilities, perception, tactics) * Social (persuasion, intimidation, leadership, deception)
  2. Pool Attribute sets how many dice you roll, divided by the table's Power Ratio (see 2.2): * PWR for Physical · AURA for Magical/Mental · CHA for Social
  3. Finesse Attribute sets your Success Threshold: * DEX for Physical · INT for Magical/Mental · CHA for Social
  4. Build the Pool: (Pool Attribute ÷ Power Ratio, round up) + situational ±1. Nothing else adds dice.
  5. Note your Assured Successes (from your one applicable Skill and any AS effects) — these are counted after the roll, not added to the pool.
  6. Roll. Count rolled successes (natural 10 = 2 successes), then add your Assured Successes — but only if you rolled at least one real success.
  7. Compare the total to the target number (Evasion, Resolve, or Difficulty).

2.2 Power Ratio (Table Dial)

The Power Ratio controls how many dice an attribute buys. It is the single knob a table turns at session zero to set overall character power. Pool dice = Pool Attribute ÷ Power Ratio (round up).

Dial Ratio ATT 1 ATT 2 ATT 3 ATT 4 ATT 5 Feel
Heroic 1:1 1 2 3 4 5 Big, reliable pools — pulpy, forgiving
Standard 2:1 1 1 2 2 3 Recommended. Lean pools; AS and Margin carry growth
Gritty 3:1 1 1 1 2 2 Sparse dice; every success matters; lethal
  • Round up: at Standard, ATT 3 → 2 dice (3÷2 = 1.5 → 2).
  • The dial applies to PC pools only. Enemy pools (§14) are fixed numbers calibrated at Standard — see §14 for Heroic/Gritty enemy adjustments.
  • Because growth flows through Assured Successes, rerolls, threshold, flat damage, and Margin, even a Heroic level-cap character rarely rolls more than ~5 dice. That is the point.

2.3 Success Threshold Scale

All attributes run 1–5. Each point is a meaningful jump.

Finesse Attribute Success Threshold Per-Die Chance Entropy Steps Down On
1 6+ 50% 1–5
2 5+ 60% 1–4
3 4+ 70% 1–3
4 3+ 80% 1–2
5 2+ 90% 1 only

The GM may shift the threshold ±1 for strong fictional positioning or severe hardship (−1 is better, +1 is worse).

2.4 Difficulty (Non-Combat)

Difficulty Successes Needed Example
Easy 1 Climb a knotted rope
Moderate 2 Pick a standard lock
Hard 3 Leap a collapsing bridge
Extreme 4 Recall a secret only three people know

2.5 Margin

Margin = total successes − target number, capped at AR/2 (minimum 1, maximum 4).

  • The cap scales with the actor's Ability Rank: AR 1–3 → 1, AR 4–5 → 2, AR 6–7 → 3, AR 8+ → 4. This is how a veteran's good rolls land harder without rolling more dice — the pool stays small, the payoff grows.
  • In combat, Margin adds to damage (see 6.4).
  • Out of combat, Margin buys quality: faster, quieter, extra information, style.
  • Margin 0 is still a success — barely.

Global Rules

  • Always round down — except Pool dice, which round up (so a 1-attribute always rolls at least 1 die).
  • Only one Skill applies per roll (it grants Assured Successes, not dice — see §4).
  • Situational dice cap at ±1. This is the only thing besides your attribute that changes pool size.
  • No character effect adds dice. Feats, traits, conditions, and abilities grant Assured Successes, rerolls, or flat damage. (The sole exception is the Channeler — its Read and React and Overcharge mechanics are the one specialization built around adding dice, traded against Entropy risk; see §13.)
  • Pool floor: 1 die. Dice penalties (Heavy, Off-Balance, Staggered, Terrified, etc.) never reduce a pool below 1 die — because pools are small now, a penalty thins your odds rather than erasing your turn. (Penalties also still stack their other effects.)
  • The one-success guard: Assured Successes only count if you roll at least one real success. Roll all failures and your AS evaporate — you cannot win on guaranteed successes alone.

3. Character Statistics

3.1 Attributes

Attributes run 1–5. Characters start at 1 in all five. Every attribute has exactly one offensive job and one defensive job — no attribute does double duty in the same arena.

Attribute Offensive Role Defensive Role
PWR Physical pool size (÷ Power Ratio) Grit bonus (Grit = 6 + AR + PWR)
DEX Physical Success Threshold; Initiative Evasion = 1 + DEX/2
INT Magical/Mental Success Threshold Entropy stability
AURA Magical/Mental pool size (÷ Power Ratio) Magic Resistance = AURA/2
CHA Social pool size (÷ Power Ratio) AND threshold Resolve = 1 + CHA/2

Why CHA is allowed both social roles: social conflict is symmetric — your CHA offense meets the target's CHA-based Resolve. The double role is self-balancing, and a high CHA buys a small pool through the Power Ratio just like any other pool attribute.

3.2 Ability Rank (AR)

AR is the overall power tier of a character.

  • Base AR = (PWR + DEX + INT + AURA) / 2 (round down)
  • AR may also be bought directly with CCP. AR bonuses stack unless stated otherwise.
AR Ability Slots
1–3 1
4–6 2
7–9 3
10–12 4
13+ 5

Magic Resistance AURA/2, + gear — Elemental & Magical

4. Skills

A Skill is practiced competence in a domain. Skills grant Assured Successes — guaranteed successes counted with your roll. Training makes you reliably good without handing you more dice to roll, which is what keeps pools flat as characters advance.

4.1 Training Levels

Training Assured Successes CCP Cost
Untrained +0 —
Trained +1 1
Expert +2 3
Master +3 5
  • A Skill's AS count only if you roll ≥1 real success (the one-success guard, §2 Global Rules). Training sharpens a hit; it cannot manufacture one from a whiff.
  • Only one Skill may apply to a roll. Skills also gate Feat access (see 11).

4.2 Skill Domains

4.3 Armor Mastery (Special Training, Martial Domain)

Armor Mastery does not add dice. It removes armor penalties (see 7.1).

Tier CCP Benefit

Expert 3 Reduce armor Evasion/Initiative penalty by 1
Master 5 No penalties from any armor

5. Survival Resources

5.1 Grit

Short-term endurance: exertion, bruises, morale, the will to keep fighting.

  • Grit = 6 + AR + PWR
  • Damage applies to Grit first. Restores fully after every scene.

5.2 Health

Lasting harm: deep wounds, fractures, trauma.

  • Health = 2 + (AR / 2)
  • Recovers slowly — downtime, care, or magic (setting-dependent).

5.3 Dying

At 0 Health you are Down — unconscious and dying. You die if:

  • you take any further damage, or
  • the scene ends before an ally stabilizes you (Moderate Medicine check — 2 successes — or any Heal Health effect).

A stabilized character wakes at 1 Health after the scene with +2 Exhaustion.

5.4 Exhaustion & Recovery

Level Name Effect
1 Strained −1 die to all pools
2 Weary +1 Success Threshold
3 Spent Cannot use Abilities
4 Broken Max Grit = 0, half speed
5 Death's Door Unconscious; any damage kills
  • Scene end: Grit restores fully.
  • Long Rest: Full Grit; recover Health per setting; remove ability locks; reduce Exhaustion by 1d4. Entropy Dice do not reset on rest.

6. Combat

6.1 Action Economy

Action Type Per Turn Description
Standard Action 1 Primary action — full, deliberate execution
Quick Action 1 Fast and efficient, done alongside other things
Reaction 1/round Outside your turn, in response to a trigger
Movement 1 Free. Move one zone.
  • Double Move: Spend your Standard Action to move one additional zone.
  • Quick Repositioning: Adjustments within your Engaged Zone are free.
  • Sustained Abilities: Activate as a Standard Action; pay a Quick Action each round to maintain or the ability ends.

6.2 Zones

Zone Distance Movement Cost
Engaged Where you are None
Adjacent One zone away 1 Move
Far Two zones 1 Move + Standard Action
Extreme Three or more zones Cannot reach in one turn

A zone is roughly the space where a small group could melee — a room, a corridor section, a treeline, a hilltop.

6.3 Initiative

No roll. Initiative = DEX + AR − armor penalty.

Tiebreakers: higher DEX, then higher AR, then PCs decide among themselves (GM decides for NPCs).

Surprise: Partial surprise → −2 Initiative for the scene start. Full ambush → ambushers each act once, surprised targets may only use a reaction; then run normal Initiative.

6.4 Attack Sequence

  1. Roll your Attack Pool at your Success Threshold.
  2. Count rolled successes (10 = 2 successes). If you rolled ≥1, add your Assured Successes (Skill + AS effects). If you rolled 0, the attack misses outright — AS cannot save it.
  3. Compare your total successes to the defender's Evasion (or Resolve for mental attacks): * Total < target → Miss * Total ≥ target → Hit
  4. Damage = Flat Damage (weapon or ability) + Margin − applicable reduction, where Margin = total successes − target, capped at AR/2 (min 1, max 4). * Physical: subtract Armor · Magical/Elemental: subtract MR · Psychological/Ephemeral: no reduction
  5. Apply damage to Grit first, overflow to Health.
  6. Resolve Impact Riders.

Worked example (Standard dial). PWR 3 → 2 dice (3÷2 round up). DEX 2 → threshold 5+. Trained Martial = +1 Assured Success. AR 3 → Margin cap 1. Roll: 8, 4 → 1 rolled success (the 8). ≥1, so the +1 AS counts → 2 total successes. Target Evasion 2 → hit. Margin = 2 − 2 = 0. Common sword (2) + Margin 0 = 2 damage − Armor 1 = 1 damage to Grit.

Same character, hotter roll: 10, 6 → 2 + 1 = 3 rolled, +1 AS = 4 total. Margin = 4 − 2 = 2, but capped at AR/2 = 1. Sword 2 + 1 = 3 − Armor 1 = 2 damage, and the natural 10 fires the Brutal rider. Note the pool never grew — the roll and the cap did the scaling.

6.5 Impact Riders

Rider Trigger Effect
Glancing Hit, but final damage = 0 Target is Off-Balance (−1 die next roll)
Overwhelm Final damage ≥ 4 Target is Staggered (−2 dice, no Double Move, 1 round)
Brutal Hit, and the roll contains a natural 10 Choose until your next turn: Sundered Plates (target Armor −1, min 0) or Cracked Guard (target Evasion −1, min 0)

8.5 Difficulty Value & the Entropy Die

DV = (Stability Base + Total Effect Cost) − AR

Stability Base World
10 High Magic — stable, safe, even novices rarely face backlash
20 Standard — magic is a learned science with clear risks (recommended)
30 Grim/Volatile — wild and dangerous; even masters fear a bad roll
DV Starting Entropy Die
0–10 d20 (Perfectly Stable)
11–20 d12 (Reliable)
21–30 d10 (Standard)
31–40 d8 (Volatile)
41–50 d6 (Dangerous)
51+ d4 (Imminent Backlash)

After each use, roll the ability's Entropy Die. If the result falls below your INT Success Threshold (the same threshold from 2.3), the ability works but the die steps down: d20 → d12 → d10 → d8 → d6 → d4.

Example: INT 3 → threshold 4+. Entropy steps down on 1–3 (30%). One investment, two payoffs: INT buys accuracy and stability with the same point.

Catastrophic Backlash — if the Entropy Die is a d4 and the roll falls below your threshold: * The ability fails. * Take 1 Health damage (bypasses Grit). * Gain +1 Exhaustion. * The ability is Locked until a Long Rest.

Domain Affinity: each Specialization names a category of effects that start one Entropy Die tier higher (max d20).

8.6 Ability Creation Procedure

  1. Name and describe it.
  2. Choose Nature → 3. Expression → 4. Action Type → 5. Range → 6. Targeting → 7. Effects.
  3. Total DV = (Stability Base + Effect Cost) − AR.
  4. Look up the starting Entropy Die; apply Domain Affinity if it qualifies.
  5. Note your Entropy step-down band from INT.

8.7 Worked Examples

Conflagrate (Magical · Standalone · Standard) Standard −2, Close +2, Single +0, Flat Damage (Fire) ×2 +6, Condition minor (Burn) +2 → Cost +8, DV = (20+8) − AR. Roll AURA vs INT threshold. Successes ≥ Evasion: 2 Fire damage + Margin, reduced by MR; target gains Burn.

Spectral Echo (Hybrid · Follow-up · Standard) Standard −2, Follow-up +4, Touch +0, Single +0, Flat Damage (Arcane) ×2 +6 → Cost +8. When your weapon attack hits, roll AURA vs INT threshold; deal 2 Arcane + Margin, reduced by MR. No weapon traits or riders apply to the echo.

Warlord's Command (Social · Standalone · Quick) Quick +0, Close +2, Single +0, Move/Reposition +2, Assured Success ×1 (granted) +4 → Cost +8. One ally moves one zone free and gains +1 Assured Success on their next roll. Willing allies don't check Resolve.

Mind Shatter (Mental · Standalone · Standard) Standard −2, Medium +4, Single +0, Flat Damage (Psychological) ×3 +9, Condition major (Shaken) +4 → Cost +15. Roll AURA vs INT threshold against the target's Resolve. On a hit: 3 Psychological damage + Margin, unreduced; target is Shaken.

  • WP (Weapon Points): weapon traits.

9.4 Procedure

  1. Concept — name, background, role.
  2. Specialization — choose one (0 CCP) + its free Spec Feat.
  3. Spend CCP — attributes, skills, traits, feats.
  4. Spend EP — armor, shield, weapon tier upgrade.
  5. Spend WP — weapon traits (remaining EP converts 1:1, max 3).
  6. Build Abilities — slots from AR (3.2).
  7. Audit — verify budgets, compute Grit, Health, Evasion, Resolve, MR, Initiative, AR.

10. Traits — Who You Are

Innate qualities. Always passive, always on. 2 CCP each, no prerequisites, no repeats.

Physical

  • Ironhide — +1 Natural Armor permanently. Cannot combine with Grit Guard.
  • Nimble — +1 Evasion permanently.
  • Grit Guard — While you have Grit, +2 Natural Armor and +1 Evasion. All of it vanishes at 0 Grit.
  • Last Step — While you have Grit, +1 Evasion. Lost at 0 Grit.
  • Ambidextrous — No off-hand penalties. While dual wielding, +1 flat damage on attacks.
  • Quickfoot — Reroll one failed die on movement rolls; act before non-Quickfoot characters at tied Initiative; move through occupied zones freely.

Mental

  • Extreme Focus — At the start of your turn, roll 1d10. That number is an additional Critical Success value for your rolls this turn (in addition to 10s).
  • Unshakeable — Immune to morale and fear conditions; intimidation auto-fails against you. −1 die on all CHA rolls.
  • Pain Tolerance — Act normally at 0 Grit until you take Health damage.
  • Instinct — Cannot be fully ambushed; always act in surprise rounds. Once per scene, reroll one Initiative-dependent check.

Background

  • Unnatural Vitality — Ignore the first level of Exhaustion gained each day.
  • Survivor — At 1 Health: +1 Assured Success on all rolls, and ignore the first source of further Health damage each scene.
  • Hardened — Psychological conditions and fear cannot reduce your dice pools. −1 die on Social rolls.
  • Road Worn — Harsh environments and scarcity never impose roll penalties. Reroll one failed die on Survival and navigation.

Presence

  • Commanding Presence — Allies within 2 zones may reroll one failed die vs fear/morale. Once per scene, grant one ally an immediate out-of-turn reaction.
  • Unsettling — Enemies' first roll against you each scene is at +1 Success Threshold. Reroll one failed die on intimidate; −1 die to comfort or persuade warmly.
  • Blood-Price Marked — Enemies who know you've killed suffer −1 die on their first roll against you; allies who watch you fight may reroll one failed die on their next morale roll.

13. Specializations

Archetype-agnostic philosophies — a tanky mage is Resolute, a rage mage is Devastating. Choose one at creation (0 CCP). Each grants: Identity, Passive, Power (1/scene), Price, Domain Affinity, and one free Spec Feat.

The Resolute

Built to still be standing when everyone else has fallen.

  • Passive — Iron Presence: At or below half Grit: +1 Assured Success on all rolls and +1 flat damage.
  • Power — Defiant Stand: When damage would drop your Grit to 0 or below, negate that hit. Until your next turn you can't go below 1 Grit.
  • Price — The Long Game: In combat, you cannot restore Grit above half maximum by any means.
  • Domain Affinity — Endurance: Magical Armor, Magical Shield, Recoup, Heal, Duration.
  • Spec Feats (pick one): Bloodied Resolve (once/scene, on taking Health damage, gain that much Grit) · Immovable (no forced movement works on you) · Armor of Scars (+1 Natural Armor) · Threshold Mentality (first time at 0 Grit each scene: your next roll counts every die as a success).

The Precise

The battlefield is a problem you've already solved.

  • Passive — Read the Room: At the start of your turn pick a visible target: −1 Success Threshold against them until your next turn.
  • Power — Perfect Execution: Once/scene, vs your Read target: the roll auto-satisfies Evasion. Damage and effects resolve normally.
  • Price — Tunnel Vision: Everyone except your Read target gets +1 Evasion against you.
  • Domain Affinity — Control: Inflict Condition, Move/Reposition, Threshold Band, Duration.
  • Spec Feats: Exploit Weakness (+1 flat damage vs targets with any condition or reduced stat) · Setup Artist (you apply a condition → next ally vs that target may reroll one failed die) · Patient Aim (didn't move: reroll one failed die on your next roll this turn) · Pressure Points (on any Critical Success, apply a free minor condition).

The Devastating

Does this make the next hit hurt more? The answer is always yes.

  • Passive — Escalating Force: Every 5 total final damage you deal this scene: +1 flat damage on subsequent attacks. No ceiling.
  • Power — Unleash: Once/scene, before rolling: double all your flat damage bonuses on this attack.
  • Price — Burn Hot: Every time one of your Entropy Dice steps down, take 1 Health damage (bypasses Grit).
  • Domain Affinity — Destruction: Flat Damage, Damage over Time, Area.
  • Spec Feats: Overkill (excess damage beyond a kill carries to one adjacent enemy) · Momentum (drop a target to 0 Grit: +1 Assured Success on your next roll) · Rend (deal 4+ in one hit: target Armor −1 for the scene) · Volatile Output (once/scene: reroll your whole pool, take the second result).

The Channeler

You don't cast abilities — you inhabit them.

  • Passive — Read and React: Roll the Entropy Die before using an ability. If it wouldn't step down: proceed with +1 die. If it would: proceed anyway, or cancel (die doesn't move, action is spent).
  • Built-in — Overcharge: Add up to +3 dice to an ability's pool. The Entropy Die steps down on any result of X or less, where X = dice added (stacks with the normal band).
  • Power — Last Word: Once/scene: an ability auto-succeeds at maximum — every die counts as a 10. The Entropy Die then steps down (two tiers, via Brittle Precision).
  • Price — Brittle Precision: Your Entropy Dice step down two tiers instead of one.
  • Domain Affinity — Chosen Mastery: After each Long Rest, pick one ability to start a tier higher.
  • Spec Feats: Controlled Burn (take 1 Health to prevent one of the two Brittle tiers) · Expanded Attunement (+1 Ability Slot) · Echo Cast (once/scene: repeat an ability with no Entropy roll; no Last Word/Overcharge on it) · Calculated Risk (Overcharge that doesn't step down: +1 flat damage per die added).

The Architect

By the time the first blow lands, the outcome is already decided.

  • Passive — Architect's Gambit: Once/scene, before a round starts: swap the Initiative positions of one friendly and one enemy for that round.
  • Power — Pour It Through: Once/scene: one ally in your zone gains all your flat damage bonuses (gear, traits, effects) until your next turn. You make no attacks while it's active.
  • Price — The Weight of the Plan: Your own weapon counts as one tier lower (minimum Mundane).
  • Domain Affinity — Support: Heal, Recoup, extra Targets, Create/Shape, Inflict Condition.
  • Spec Feats: Coordinated Strike (once/scene: designate a target; the next two allies acting against it each get +1 Assured Success) · Disrupting Presence (enemies starting within 1 zone: +1 ST on their first roll) · Rally (once/scene: an ally who hears you removes one condition) · Interceptor (once/scene: redirect a hit on an adjacent ally to yourself).

14. Enemies & GM Tools

14.1 Enemy Stat Block

Every enemy lists: Pool · Threshold · Evasion · Armor · MR · Damage · Grit/Health · Margin Cap · Menace · Behavior tag.

Enemies attack exactly like PCs: roll Pool at Threshold vs the PC's Evasion; damage = Damage + Margin − PC's Armor, where the enemy's Margin Cap stands in for AR/2 (Fodder/Standard 1, Elite 2, Boss 3). Enemy pools are fixed numbers — they do not use the Power Ratio. (Menace is only used by the optional player-facing module, 17.1.)

14.2 Threat Tiers

Calibrated per PC, against a fresh Standard-dial starting character (Common weapon, 2-die pool + 1 Skill AS, threshold 5+, Evasion 1–2).

Tier Target Rounds Pool Threshold Evasion Armor MR Damage Grit / Health Margin Cap Menace
Fodder ~2 2 6+ 1 0 0 1 3 / 1 1 1
Standard ~4 3 5+ 2 1 0 2 6 / 2 1 2
Elite 6–8 5 4+ 3 1 1 3 10 / 4 2 3
Boss 10+ 7 4+ 4 2 2 4 15 / 6 3 4

Design per-PC, multiply enemy count by party size. Elites and Bosses assume on-tier (advanced) PCs or a coordinated party.

Dial adjustments (match the table to your PCs' Power Ratio): * Heroic (1:1): PCs roll fatter pools and hit more reliably — give Standard-and-up enemies +1 Evasion and +2 Grit so fights still last their target rounds. * Standard (2:1): use the table as written. * Gritty (3:1): PCs roll sparse pools — give enemies −1 Evasion (minimum 1) so PCs aren't whiffing for whole scenes; leave damage and Grit alone (lethality is the point).

14.3 Behavior Tags

  • Aggressive: focuses wounded or isolated targets.
  • Tactical: uses cover, retreats, targets casters and support.
  • Relentless: one free attack when reduced to 0 Grit.
  • Protector: may take a hit for an adjacent ally.

14.4 Difficulty Presets

  • Low Danger: simpler enemies, Stability Base 10.
  • Standard: baseline, Stability Base 20.
  • Brutal: smart enemies, hidden traps, Stability Base 30.

15. Advancement

Level-Ups are awarded at major milestones (recommended: every 1–3 sessions).

Each Level-Up grants:

  • +2 Grit
  • +1 Health on odd-numbered Level-Ups
  • +1 AR on even-numbered Level-Ups
  • CCP: Slow +3 · Standard +5 · Fast +7

Level-Up CCP buys anything from the creation table (9.2). Attribute cap 5 always applies.

Alternate awards (GM discretion): +1 CCP for a standout scene, +2 for a major objective or boss, +3 for an arc climax.

16. Exploration

16.1 Exploration Turns

1 Exploration Turn = 10 minutes. Each turn: Party Action (Move / Search / Force / Interact / Short Rest — no Health recovery) → Consequences Tick (light burns, noise builds, timers advance) → pressure roll.

  • Careful Search: +1 turn, −1 Success Threshold on the roll.
  • Rush: −1 turn, +1 Success Threshold.
  • Torch: 6 turns. Lantern: 24 turns.

16.2 Dungeon Pressure Die

Start at d12 (dangerous zones d10 or d8). At the end of each Exploration Turn, roll it:

  • On a 1: a complication occurs (encounter, hazard, alarm, loss) and the die steps down (d12→d10→d8→d6→d4).
  • On a 1 at d4: a major event, then reset to d12.
  • Relief (step the die up one): secure rest, leaving the zone, or spending real resources to stabilize.

17. Optional Modules

17.1 Player-Facing Defense (AI-GM / solo play)

For tables where players roll all dice (the standard UDS playtest protocol), enemies never roll. When an enemy attacks:

  1. The defending player rolls a Defense Check: pool = DEX ÷ Power Ratio (dodge) or PWR ÷ Power Ratio (brace — requires a shield or weapon in hand), at their Physical threshold. A shield grants +1 Assured Success to brace, and a defensive Skill grants its AS as normal.
  2. Total successes ≥ enemy Menace → the attack misses.
  3. Otherwise: take enemy Damage + shortfall (Menace − total successes, capped at the enemy's Margin Cap) − your Armor.

This is the mirror of 14.1 — the player rolls to dodge instead of the enemy rolling to hit. Use one mode or the other for a whole scene, never both.

17.2 Social Conflict

Social pressure is an attack vs Resolve: roll CHA pool at CHA threshold; successes ≥ Resolve means the target genuinely shifts. Margin sets the size of the shift (Margin 0: a small concession; Margin at your cap: meaningful cooperation).

  • PCs are never mind-controlled. A social hit on a PC imposes a condition (e.g., Rattled, Shaken) or a complication, never a scripted action — the player decides how the pressure lands.
  • Repeated identical attempts on the same target don't roll; change the leverage or accept the answer.
