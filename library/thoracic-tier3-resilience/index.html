Thoracic Spine Recovery · Tier 3

Resilience & return to load

Build robust pulling strength plus loaded thoracic extension and rotation for a durable mid-back. Five advanced moves, Tonal-led.

Return-to-load Tonal-led Rhomboid overload Loaded extension & rotation

The Short Version

How to use this tier

At a glance

  • This is the return-to-load tier. Enter only when you are fully symptom-free and Tier 2 is clean.
  • Set the shoulder blade before every pull. Depress and retract first, then let the arms work — this protects the weak rhomboid pattern.
  • Load progresses in small steps. Clean form gates every increase; a flare means back off that movement.

The Five Moves

Tier 3 exercises

01

Pull-Up / Lat Pulldown

Targets
Lats, lower traps, rhomboids; loaded thoracic extension.
Setup / How-to
From the rack pull-up bar, use a band for assistance as needed; initiate by depressing and retracting the shoulder blades before the arms pull. Tonal option: arms set high, bar attachment, kneeling lat pulldown to the upper chest, squeezing the blades; full control on the way up and down.
Dosage
5–8 reps × 3 (assisted as needed).
Avoid
Dead-hang yanking; shrugging at the bottom; over-arching / rib flare at the top.
Caution
Reintroduce vertical pulling only when pain-free; the initial scapular set protects the rhomboid pattern.
02

Tonal Standing Reverse Fly

Targets
Rhomboids, rear delts, mid-traps under higher load.
Setup / How-to
Tonal, dual handles. Arms set mid/high, handles crossed, slight hinge. Open the arms out and back in a wide arc, squeezing the shoulder blades hard at the end; slow return. Moderate weight; use Eccentric/Chains for overload once solid.
Dosage
10–12 reps × 3.
Avoid
Swinging / momentum; shrugging; bending the elbows into a row.
Caution
This directly overloads the previously weak rhomboid — add load only with clean form.
03

Tonal Bent-Over Row

Targets
Whole mid-back, rhomboids, lats — heavier horizontal pull.
Setup / How-to
Tonal, bar. Hinge at the hips with a flat back, bar attachment. Row to the lower ribs / navel, elbows back, squeeze the blades, control down. Heavier load; Spotter / Smart Flex on.
Dosage
8–10 reps × 3.
Avoid
Rounding the back; jerking up with the low back; shrugging.
Caution
Brace the trunk and keep the ribs stacked — avoids loading a flared / extended position.
04

Tonal Cable Rotation / Chop

Targets
Loaded thoracic rotation, obliques, integrated trunk strength.
Setup / How-to
Tonal, single handle, arm set mid; stand side-on to the unit. Rotate the trunk away from the unit driving from the mid-back, arms relatively straight; control the return. Moderate weight.
Dosage
10–12 per side × 2–3.
Avoid
Rotating only from the low back / knees; yanking; over-twisting.
Caution
Earn loaded rotation — only after Tier 2 rotation mobility is pain-free at T6–T7.
05

Tonal Cable Pullover

Targets
Lats, serratus, loaded thoracic extension + ribcage expansion.
Setup / How-to
Tonal, rope or bar, arms set high. Standing or kneeling with arms relatively straight, pull the handle down in an arc toward the hips while letting the ribcage expand and the mid-back extend, then control back overhead. Moderate, slow.
Dosage
10–12 reps × 3.
Avoid
Bending the elbows into a pulldown; over-arching the low back; rib flare.
Caution
Keep the motion in the mid-back, not the lumbar spine — this trains extension under load safely.

Read before loading

Safety

  • Enter this tier only when Tier 2 is clean and fully symptom-free.
  • Still set the shoulder blade before every pull; clean form gates every load increase.
  • Keep ribs stacked / down and brace the trunk under load — protects the costochondral joints and T6–T7.
  • Progress load in small steps; a flare means back off that movement.
  • Be cautious with heavy overhead pressing and dips (they provoke costochondritis).
  • Given the prior acute subluxation, clear heavier loaded work with your clinician.

Disclaimer

This plan is educational and not a substitute for individualized assessment. Given the prior acute T6–T7 subluxation and ongoing costochondritis, clear heavier loaded work — especially loaded rotation, extension, and heavy pulling — with your clinician before progressing. Let symptoms, not the calendar, lead.
